## Exam Tips

Advice from people who passed.

### Before the Exam

1. **Complete pre-licensing first** - Most states require education before the exam
2. **Know your state's quirks** - State-specific questions vary
3. **Focus on definitions** - Many questions test terminology
4. **Understand policy types** - Know the differences between products

### Life & Health Specific

- **Life insurance:** Know term vs. permanent, cash value, death benefit taxation
- **Health insurance:** Understand deductibles, coinsurance, HIPAA, ACA
- **Annuities:** Fixed vs. variable vs. indexed, surrender charges
- **Medicare:** Parts A, B, C, D - this is heavily tested

### Property & Casualty Specific

- **Property:** Replacement cost vs. ACV, coinsurance penalty
- **Liability:** CGL coverage parts, occurrence vs. claims-made
- **Auto:** Liability limits, uninsured/underinsured, no-fault states
- **Commercial:** BOP, workers comp, surety bonds

### During the Exam

- **Read carefully** - Insurance questions often have tricky wording
- **Eliminate wrong answers** - Usually 2 are obviously wrong
- **Watch for absolutes** - "Always" and "never" are often wrong
- **Trust your prep** - If you studied, you know this
