### Must-Know Terms

**Life & Health:**
- **Grace Period** - Time to pay premium after due date
- **Incontestability Clause** - 2-year limit on contesting claims
- **Beneficiary** - Person who receives death benefit
- **Cash Surrender Value** - Amount received if policy cancelled

**Property & Casualty:**
- **Subrogation** - Insurer's right to recover from third party
- **Indemnity** - Restoring to pre-loss condition
- **Insurable Interest** - Financial stake in insured property
- **Proximate Cause** - Direct cause of loss

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How hard is the insurance exam?

Insurance exams have a **60-70% pass rate**. Most candidates pass with 1-2 weeks of focused study after completing pre-licensing education. The exams test understanding of concepts, not just memorization.

### How long should I study for the insurance exam?

Plan for **20-40 hours of study time** after completing pre-licensing courses. Focus on weak areas and practice questions.

### Which is harder: Life & Health or Property & Casualty?

**Life & Health** is often considered slightly harder due to more complex products (annuities, variable life). Property & Casualty has more memorization but simpler concepts.

### Can I get both licenses at once?

**Yes** - many agents get both L&H and P&C licenses. You'll need to take separate exams and complete separate pre-licensing courses.

### Do I need a sponsor to get an insurance license?

**No** - unlike securities licenses, you don't need a company sponsor to take the insurance exam. You can get licensed independently.

### How much do insurance agents make?

Average insurance agent salary is **$50,000-$80,000**, with top performers earning $100,000+. Earnings depend on commission structure and sales volume.

### What's the difference between captive and independent agents?

- **Captive agents** work for one insurance company (State Farm, Allstate)
- **Independent agents** represent multiple companies
